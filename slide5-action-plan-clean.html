Slide 5 — Decision

Action Plan

With budget for one major improvement initiative, I would focus on the largest error source: same-brand SKU confusion.

The Initiative

What I Would Choose

Target and re-label the data for visually similar products from the same brand.

  • Collect more real field images for the confused product pairs.
  • Review and correct labeling quality for those SKUs.
  • Add stronger reference images for each product.
  • Build a focused training set around the products the model confuses.
Rationale

Why This Initiative?

Same-brand confusion = 18% of all cases / 46% of all errors

The model identifies the brand, but doesn't distinguish well enough between SKUs inside that brand.

This is likely addressable through better labeling, targeted images, and stronger reference data — and it's the largest error bucket, so solving it cuts the total error rate the most.

Success Metrics

How I Would Measure Success

↑Correctly identifiedIncrease in correct SKU-level recognition.
↓Same-brand confusionFewer products tagged as another product from the same brand.
↑Accuracy on confused productsHigher accuracy on the product pairs that caused most errors.
↓Human reviewFewer cases needing manual validation or correction.
