From Image to Action

The system should be designed around the business workflow, not only around the recognition model.
Discovery & scope

Start With The Use Case

Start with the use case: industry, existing solutions, customer pain, target users, and financial value.

  • What problem are we solving?
  • Who is the expected customer and user?
  • Where can accurate recognition create business value?
  • Example: OOS, planogram, availability.
Data needed

Required Inputs

  • Customer context + image / video source.
  • SKU catalog: name, brand, barcode, size, package, category.
  • Clean product images + field images.
  • Similar products, angles, lighting, occlusions, packaging versions.
  • Labels: SKU and product regions.
  • Business data: planogram, inventory, orders, sales.

Build to deploy

Operational Steps

  • Define use case -> collect & label data.
  • Choose / adapt model -> fine-tune & test.
  • Add human review for validation and correction.
  • Integrate business systems and deploy MVP.
  • Monitor errors and update data continuously.

Main Challenges

Similar SKUsSmall visual differences can cause wrong recognition.
Packaging changesRequires fast catalog and image updates.
Image qualityLighting, angle, blur, and occlusion affect accuracy.
Data imbalanceRare products need extra images and labeling.
Integration & adoptionPredictions must connect to real workflows and be simple enough for users to trust.
Field data availabilityRemote onboarding may require customer cooperation or local field work to collect enough real product images.
