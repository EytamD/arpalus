Analyze a Real Business Scenario

A product recognition model was recently trained on a new product category. The following results were observed.

Observed Results

Outcome Count Share Interpretation
Correctly identified 610 61% Not reliable enough for a production business workflow; too many cases still create errors or uncertainty.
Identified as another product from the same brand 180 18% The model may recognize brand-level cues, but struggle with SKU-level differences such as size, package type, or packaging details.
Identified as another product from a different brand 90 9% Could indicate reliance on broad visual patterns, text, color, or category-level similarities instead of exact product identity.
Not identified 120 12% May point to missing catalog coverage, low image quality, or a gap between clean training images and real field conditions.

Initial Conclusions

Accuracy is the main issueWith 39% errors or unresolved cases, the model would create operational noise and reduce product trust.
Fine-grained recognition is weakThe largest error bucket is same-brand confusion, suggesting difficulty distinguishing similar SKUs.
Data quality should be investigatedI would check labeling quality, field image coverage, catalog completeness, and whether training data reflects real conditions.
