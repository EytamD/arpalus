Additional Information

Before deciding on next steps, I would request more information to understand whether the errors come from data, labels, model behavior, or real-world conditions.
1

Product Images

Check if products are visually distinct or generic with similar packaging and limited visual cues.

2

Confusion Matrix

Understand exactly which SKUs are confused with each other, and whether errors are concentrated in specific product pairs.

3

Labeling Review

Verify that training images were labeled correctly. Incorrect or inconsistent SKU labels may cause systematic errors.

4

Not-Identified Breakdown

Understand if the issue comes from poor image quality, missing catalog items, low confidence, or detection failure.

5

Store Environment

Check whether lighting, angle, blur, occlusion, or crowded environments affected the results.

6

Confidence Scores

Understand whether mistakes happened with high confidence or low confidence, and decide when manual review is needed.

7

Training Data per SKU

Check how many training images exist for each SKU, and whether rare products are under-represented compared to popular products.
